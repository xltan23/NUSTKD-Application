About Taekwondo

POOMSAE

A defined pattern of defense-and-attack motions. Students studying Kukkiwon/WT Taekwondo must learn Taegeuk forms to advance to a higher level.
Grading under Singapore Taekwondo Federation is held once every 3 months. Outstanding performers may have a chance to obtain double promotion.
In the world of Poomsae, practitioners must exhibit high level of discipline perfecting the execution of offensive and defensive moves.
Offensive moves include: Punches, Fist Strikes, Knife Stikes and different types of Kicks
Defensive moves include: Fist Blocks, Knife Blocks, Palm Blocks More information: Taekwondo Grades

KYORUGI

Kyorugi, also known as sparring, is a free-form fight between two people. There are three sparring rounds per match, and each round is 2 minutes.
During the match competitors aim to kick opponent's head and trunk area, to gain the most amount of points. The competitor who ends with the
most amount of points wins. Points will be given to opponent for illegal kicks to other parts of the body and other forbidden behavior.
In the world of Kyorugi, practitioners must showcase many kicking techniques while also demonstrating flexibility, concentration, and stamina.
Kyorugi will an assessment criteria for grading once a practitioner attains Green belt.
Practitioners may compete in local competitions after they have attained Red belt.
